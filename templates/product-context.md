# Product Context

Exactly 5 sentences. One per prompt. Keep each sentence specific — no marketing language, no adjectives you can't defend with evidence. This is the source of truth for Phase 0.1 (Internal Read).

## 1. What it does
<One sentence describing the mechanism. What does the product literally do when a user uses it?>

## 2. Who it's for
<One sentence naming the specific role or behavior. Not "everyone who works." Something like "solo founders who run ops themselves" or "PMs who write specs weekly.">

## 3. What it replaces
<One sentence naming the current workflow, tool, or workaround it displaces. Be concrete: "a Notion doc + 3 Slack threads" beats "manual processes.">

## 4. One distinctive capability
<One sentence describing the one thing it does that competitors don't, or does meaningfully better. If you can't name one, that's a signal — flag it.>

## 5. One honest constraint
<One sentence naming a real limitation: what it doesn't do, who it isn't for, where it breaks. Constraints make the rest credible.>
